Rose has created a brand for herself because she is a versatile British actress, has played more than 10 different nationalities, and has done justice to all her characters through her dynamic acting skills. Amber is again in the limelight for her role as Mika Bakhash in “Last Light.”

Early Life & Family

Amber Rose Revah was born on 24 June 1986 in London; even though she was born and brought up in the UK, her heritage is full of diversity; her mother, Riri Hewitt, comes of Polish-Jewish descent, and her father has his roots coming from a Kenyan-Indian descent, nothing much is known about her father other than his ethnicity, he had left his family when Amber was just a kid, leaving his wife to take care of five children.

Amber has four siblings, two sisters; the youngest is still in school, the other one works as a clinical assistant at a hospital in London, and both of her brothers are ski instructors who live in Switzerland.

Her grandparents had first moved to the UK and quickly built up a successful shipping business; it was her grandmother who had introduced Revah to the world of culture and theatre; she was just 8 when she went to see the “Carousel and Fiddler On The Roof” and was so overwhelmed but it that she began to cry.

As time passed, she grew up understanding the power of drama and performing and decided what she wanted to do with her life. She attended Brunel University, did her bachelor of arts in Contemporary Performance, and enrolled in the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art, a youth group, at 17; she was booked by her agent while still in university; Amber managed to complete her education and work as well.

Acting Career

Amber Rose took her first step in the acting industry by getting a role in Shamim Sarif’s “The World Unseen” and was so impressed with her performance that he offered a position to her in his next film, “I Can’t Think Straight,” which later became an award-winning LGBT cult film.

Her character Yasmin was loved and admired by all, her talent led her to work in some renowned projects like, “House of Saddam,” “Agora,” this was director Alejandro Amenabar’s independent movie which gained a lot of fame just after its release, she was also seen in a short film, “Taylors Trophy.”

It was the film “From Paris with Love” that brought out her true talent; she was seen alongside some famous faces like John Travolta and Jonathan Rhys Meyers, yet Amber managed to shine as Nichole, she had some other great films as well, “The Devil’s Double,” “Everywhere and Nowhere,” “Aazaan,” “Son of God.

Along with working on a large screen, Rose showcased her talent on the small screen as well, with some big-budget series like “Borgia,” “The Mystery of Edwin Drood,” “The Bible,” “What Remains,” “Silent Witness,” even though she was seen for only a few episodes in most of the series, with her skills and strong presence she managed to add a lot of value to the whole plot.

Her big break came in 2015 when she was a part of a year-long series, “Indian Summers,’ she quickly rose to fame and bagged noteworthy roles in “Emerald City” and “The Punisher,” she had to split her time between London and New York while shooting these series, she bagged “Concrete Plans.”

In 2021 and a year later, she is back with her most recent thriller Mini TV series, “Last Light,” she has upcoming projects as well; she shifts her genres from thriller to comedy in the film, “The Trouble with Jessica,” and also a series, “The Peripheral,” all in the same year.
