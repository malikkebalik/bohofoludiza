Meet Taylor James, Who Played Batu in “Vikings: Valhalla”

Taylor James (born on January 26, 1980) is a 42-year-old British actor from Sevenoaks, Kent. He has been in the news a lot lately because he played the part of Batu in Jeb Stuart-directed “Vikings Valhalla season 2,” which came out recently on Netflix. This “Vikings” spin-off revolves around a story of a world 100 years after and shows the adventures of William the Conqueror, Harald Hardrada, Leif Erikson, and Freydis.

James worked extensively on both small and big screens. He started his career with a show titled “Hotel Babylon” in 2007 and then debuted in the film industry with “Mamma Mia!” playing Stag.

In his more than 1.5 decades long career Taylor worked on numerous productions, including some notable mentions like “Justice League,” “Samson,” “Zack Snyder’s Justice League,” “Material Girl,” “Howard’s Happy Place,” and “The Liberator.”

How Old is Taylor James?

On January 26, 1980, Taylor James (age 42) was born in Sevenoaks, Kent, England, United Kingdom. According to sources, he belongs to a Christian family, but we do not possess much information about his parents and siblings. Tay got into acting because of his passion for action movies.

Taylor James’s Career

Taylor’s second acting job came when the casting director of “Mamma Mia!” Priscilla John picked him to portray Stag. Viewers loved Taylor’s performance in this romantic musical drama, and that opened more doors of opportunities for him.

From 2009 to 2011, he worked on various productions, including “Faith Alone,” “Blitz,” “The Power of Three,” “The L Word,” “Material Girl,” and “Sirens.” From 2012 to 2014, this 6′ 2½” British actor stayed away from movies and worked only on shows, namely “Red Dwarf” and “Howard’s Happy Place.”

In 2015 he returned to the movie world with Rob Ashford’s “The Winter’s Tale.” The same year he played Nick in Mitch Davis’s comedy film “Christmas Eve” and gathered accolades.

He was also in the star-studded “Justice League” but played an uncredited role which is why not many people noticed him. With “Vikings: Valhalla,” Taylor’s fame reached heights, and he earned the star status that he had always wished for.
